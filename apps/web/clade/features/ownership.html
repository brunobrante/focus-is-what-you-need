Plate 04 one mechanism, three verbs

Threads you can trust.

Components, design tokens, references — everything Clade shares flows through one mechanism: linkable → instance → detach.

the problem

In most tools you can’t tell a copy from a reference until one breaks.

Overrides on top of overrides. Detached instances that look attached. A library update that silently rewrites forty screens on a Tuesday. Canvas tools blur the line between the real thing and its reflections — and you find out which is which at the worst moment.

Deleting a shared component is a small act of vandalism: somewhere, someone’s file just lost a piece.

the clade way

Mark it linkable. Place an instance. Or detach — and own it.

A master marked linkable can be placed anywhere below it as a linked instance — a live reference, read-only by design. It shows the master’s content; it cannot lie to you. To edit, you go to the master at its home, and a back button rounds the trip exactly to where you were.

Detach cuts the thread: the instance becomes a true local copy, fully yours, connected to nothing. The same three verbs work for components, tokens, and references alike.

how the component model dissolves it

Nobody’s screen breaks silently.

Unlink or delete a master that is still placed elsewhere, and Clade asks — per instance — what should happen there: keep a copy (detach, the default) or delete it too. Every place gets a deliberate answer; no place gets a surprise.

Ownership and origin are never ambiguous: you can always tell whether you’re looking at a reference or the real thing, and where the real thing lives.
