Plate 06 a side tool, on purpose

Cut pictures into trees.

A standalone tool that turns static UI images — or frames pulled from video — into stacks: trees of cuts that remember exactly where they came from.

the problem

Your references are flat. Your work is structured.

A great Dribbble shot can inspire a layout, but it can’t be dissected. It’s pixels: the header, the card, the button are fused into one image, and you re-derive the structure by eye every time you glance at it.

Reference boards live in one app, structure in another, and nothing connects them to the component you’re actually building.

the clade way

Cut the screen out. Then keep cutting.

Upload an image and it becomes a screen. Cut out buttons, texts, sections — every cut lands positioned where it was cut from, and together they rebuild the original as a stack. Several screens form a group; a video becomes frames, and frames become screens.

Three windows, one focus each: the Builder for the cut in hand, the Stack for the whole, the Gallery for the tree in order.

how the component model dissolves it

A reference with structure is a reference you can share by the piece.

Stacks are references — so they flow through the same linkable mechanism as everything else. Share a whole image, a stack, or one cut with its children into any project, screen, or component. Detach it there if you need to own it.

Per-cut AI tools — background removal, upscale, element removal — save variants while keeping the original, so an edited cut and its source live side by side in the tree.
