Plate 03 the rhythm

Drill, don’t pan.

Whole screen → one isolated child → that child’s children beside it → repeat. That rhythm is the heart of Clade.

the problem

Finding one button in a big file is an expedition.

Scroll. Pan. Zoom out until your screens are confetti. Squint. Zoom back in on the wrong frame. Canvas tools eventually bolted on “jump to element” — a patch that admits the core model is a hunt.

Starting is no better: a blank infinite sheet gives you nothing to begin from, and organizing it is your job before you’ve created anything at all.

the clade way

The tree is the map — and the map is the territory.

Opening a screen shows the complete composition: every visible child already in place, the whole layout readable in one look. Click a component and it becomes the subject: shown alone, at its real size, never mixed back into the screen.

Its children appear adjacent — beside it, ready to be opened next. One focused level at a time, as deep as the design goes.

how the component model dissolves it

The parent is the spine, not metadata.

Every node knows its screen, its parent, and its children — one hierarchy feeds previews, detail views, child lists, and navigation alike. The breadcrumb path is always intact, and stepping back is one crumb away.

“I started at Home, saw the whole screen, clicked Header, saw Header by itself, then inspected Logo — and I never lost track of where I was.” If that sentence stays true, navigation is correct. In Clade, it stays true.

the result
