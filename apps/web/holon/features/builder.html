Deep dive · 05

From flat shot to living stack.

Inspiration usually arrives as dead pixels — a Dribbble shot, a Behance frame, a screenshot. The Builder gives it structure.

The problem

References you can't reach into

You found the perfect header on Dribbble. Now what? It’s a flat image: you can look at it, you can pin it to a moodboard, but you can’t isolate the part you actually care about, and it certainly can’t travel with the component you’re building.

Inspiration and work live in different universes, and you’re the ferry.

The approach

Cut it apart, keep it whole

Drop a UI shot into the Builder and cut out the screen — it becomes the main of a new stack. Keep cutting: the header, the button, the list. Every cut lands exactly where it was cut from, so together the pieces rebuild the original image — as a tree.

One image can hold several screens; cut each one out. Multiple screens form a group automatically. You can even import a video, pick frames, and turn each frame into a screen.

The model

Stacks are references — so they flow

A stack lives in your References, and references share like everything in Holon: mark it linkable, place it into a project, a screen, or a component as a linked instance — the whole image, the whole stack, or one single cut with its children. Detach it to own a local copy.

Per-cut AI tools — background removal, upscale, element removal — save each edit as a new variant while keeping the original, so a cut carries its own history.

The result

Inspiration that speaks component

The reference for your header sits beside your header — not a whole screenshot, just the piece that matters, with its own sub-pieces intact. What you found and what you’re making finally share a structure.

Steal like a designer. Then build like one.
