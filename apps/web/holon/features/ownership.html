Deep dive · 04

Yours, linked, or let go.

Components, design tokens, references — Holon shares them all through one mechanism, with one promise: you always know what you own.

The problem

Fuzzy masters, silent breakage

Every design tool has some story for reuse, and almost every story is fuzzy at the edges: copies that secretly stay linked, instances you can half-edit, and libraries where deleting one master quietly breaks forty screens on someone else’s file.

When you can’t tell a reference from the real thing, every edit is a small gamble.

The approach

Three verbs, everywhere

Mark an item linkable, place it anywhere below as a linked instance, detach it to own an independent local copy. That’s the entire vocabulary — and it applies identically to components, System Design tokens, and references.

A linked instance is a live reflection of the master, read-only in place. To edit it you go to the master at its home — one click in, and a back button returns you exactly where you were.

Ownership and origin are never ambiguous. [LAW]
The model

Nothing breaks in silence

Unlink or delete a master that’s still placed elsewhere, and Holon asks — per instance — what should happen: keep a copy there (detach it), or delete it there too. No orphaned ghosts, no surprise holes in someone’s screen.

Versions obey the same honesty. A linked version follows its masters; a copy version owns every part outright, with zero secret ties to the originals. Promote any version to main and the components physically move to the new owner — the crown, never just the label.

The result

Reuse you can trust

You can always tell whether you’re looking at the master or its reflection, and where the real thing lives. Shared buttons, brand tokens, and reference stacks flow downward through the same three verbs — and no edit is ever a gamble again.
