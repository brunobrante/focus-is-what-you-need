01 The problem

Why Holon exists

The infinite canvas promises freedom. It delivers housekeeping.

You came to design. Instead you pan, zoom, arrange, align, and tidy — because a free canvas makes organization your job. You can’t edit one thing in isolation without a ceremony. You hunt for what you need. And a blank infinite sheet gives you nothing to begin from. The price of all of it is the only thing that matters:

Focus is what you need.

02 The law of formation

Componentization, automatic

If it has a child, it’s a component.

In Holon you never declare a component. No selecting, no right-click ritual, no ⌘⌥K. The moment an element contains another element, it is a component — openable, editable, versionable, on its own.

The tree you navigate is never a diagram someone had to maintain. It’s the honest structure you actually built — atoms, molecules, organisms — formed for you as you work, not by you after hours.

04 The surface

Bounded by design

The frame is the canvas.

Whatever you open — a screen, a button, anything between — its top element becomes the frame: the surface you paint on. Everything you create lives inside it. There is nothing to edit outside it, so there is nothing outside to distract you.

Zoom in and Holon gives you elbow room around the frame. Zoom out and it stops at 1× — because at 1× your subject already fills the canvas. There is no “out there.” That’s the point.

05 Ownership

Linkable · instance · detach

One mechanism for everything shared.

Components, design tokens, references — when Holon shares something downward, it always shares it the same way. Three verbs, everywhere. And ownership is never ambiguous: you always know whether you’re holding the master or its reflection.

Master

Button / Primary

Lives at its home — the workspace. Marked linkable, so any project or screen below can place it.

Owned · editable here
Linked instance

Button / Primary

A live reference, not a copy. Read-only in place — edit the master and every instance follows. One click takes you to its home, and a back button returns you exactly here.

Linked · edit at the master
Detached

Button / Primary

The thread is cut. Now a fully independent local copy, owned by this screen. Change it, break it, version it — the master never feels a thing.

06 The supporting cast

Focused windows, focused jobs

Everything else earns its place.

Sketch

Freedom, quarantined.

One window keeps the giant free sheet — same tools, no frame, per project, local to you. Experiment wildly, then copy the good parts into the real structure. Nothing escapes on its own.

Scratchpad · never auto-promoted

Fast Edit

Cosmetics at the speed of thought.

Open any screen or component in a modal and change text, color, background, border, radius — done. It edits the real scene, but structure and geometry stay untouchable. When you need more, the canvas is the tool.

Real edits · surface only

Versions

Copies that keep their word.

Version any screen or component — linked to follow the masters, or copy to own every part outright. Promote any version to main and the components move with the crown. Never just a label swap.

Linked or copy · promotion re-homes

07 The Builder

From inspiration to structure

Cut any shot into a stack.

That screen you saved from Dribbble is a flat, dead image. The Builder brings it to life: cut out the screen, then the header, the button, the list — every cut lands exactly where it came from, and together they rebuild the original as a stack: a reference with real structure.

Link a whole stack — or a single cut — into any project, screen, or component. Pull frames from a video and turn them into screens. Inspiration finally speaks the same language as your work.
