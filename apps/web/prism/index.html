Why Prism exists

The infinite canvas is infinite housekeeping.

P·01

Disorganized by default

Arranging artboards is not designing. On a free canvas, keeping things tidy is your job — forever.

P·02

Nothing is editable alone

To work on one part in isolation you first perform the make-it-a-component ceremony. Focus shouldn't need a ritual.

P·03

Finding things is a hunt

Pan, zoom, squint, repeat. A tool should take you to the thing — not make you search an endless plane for it.

P·04

Blank means lost

An empty infinite sheet gives you nowhere to start. Structure should exist from the very first element.

The cost of all of it is focus.

One rule instead of a canvas

If an element has a child, it is a component. 

← component, automatically<div id="button">
  <div id="label">Buy now</div>

That's the entire rule. No “create component”. No ceremony, no maintenance. The structure you build becomes structure you can open, edit, version and share — and the tree of your design is always an honest reflection of what you actually made.

01 · Componentization

Components form themselves.

You never declare a component. The moment an element contains another, it becomes one — openable, editable, versionable, shareable. Atoms grow into molecules grow into organisms, and the hierarchy is built for you, never by you.

Open the feature →

02 · The Frame

A canvas with edges.

Open any component and its own boundary becomes the editing surface — nothing exists outside it. You can't zoom out below 1×, because there is no “out there”. Grids projected from its ancestors keep you aligned with a parent you can't even see.

Open the feature →

03 · Navigation

Whole screen. One child. Deeper.

Open a screen and read the entire composition. Click a section and it becomes the subject — alone, at true size, its children waiting beside it for the next step inward. However deep you go, the path home never breaks.

Open the feature →

04 · Reuse & versions

Link, don't copy-paste-pray.

One mechanism for all reuse: mark it linkable, place it as a live instance, detach it when you want to own it. Components, design tokens, references — shared the same way, with ownership that is never, ever ambiguous.

05 · The Builder

Turn inspiration into structure.

Drop in any UI screenshot — Dribbble, Behance, a screen recording — and cut it into a living stack: the screen, its sections, its buttons, each piece positioned exactly where it was cut from. Pull frames out of a video and turn them into screens.

Clean cuts up with AI — every edit saves a variant and keeps the original — then link any piece straight into your work as a reference: a whole image, a stack, or one single cut.

Focused side windows

Every job gets its own room.

Sketch

A scratchpad, on purpose

One free canvas per project — no frames, no rules, nothing leaking into your structure. Try things wildly, then paste the good ones into the real frame. Freedom belongs in a window, not at the core.

Fast Edit

Cosmetics at thought speed

Open anything in a modal and change text, colors, borders, radius — real edits to the real scene, persisted instantly. It cannot touch structure or geometry. That limitation is the feature.

References

Inspiration, kept beside you

Link the same reference into a workspace, a project, a screen, or one component — and view it side by side while you work. Stacks from the Builder come along, down to a single cut.
