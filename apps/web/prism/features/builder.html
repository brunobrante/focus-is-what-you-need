05 / 05 · The Builder

From screenshot to structure.

Inspiration usually dies in a folder. The Builder turns any static UI image — or any frame of a video — into a stack of cuts that mirrors how the interface is actually built.

The problem

References are dead pixels

You collect beautiful screens from Dribbble and Behance, and then… they sit there. Flat images. You can't isolate that button you loved, can't put the nav side by side with yours, can't reuse any piece of the thing.

Moodboards inspire for a minute and get abandoned, because a JPEG has no structure to work with.

The approach

Cut it like it was built

Upload an image and it becomes a screen. Cut out the sections, the cards, the buttons — each cut stays positioned exactly where it came from, and together they form a stack that reconstructs the original. The same decomposition Prism applies to your designs, applied to your inspiration.

1 image → 1 screen. Several screens → a group. A video → frames → screens.
Componentization at work
Standalone tool · connected through references

Cuts that flow into your work

Stacks live in your reference library and travel through the same linkable mechanism as everything else in Prism — link a whole image, a stack, or one single cut into a project, a screen, or a component, and view it beside your frame while you work:

  • Per-cut AI tools — background removal, upscaling, element erasing. Every edit saves a variant and keeps the original, so a cut carries its own history.
  • Three focused windows — Cut, where you slice one piece at a time; Stack, the layered whole; Gallery, every cut of the tree in order.
  • Video frames → screens — scrub a screen recording, pick the frames that matter, and each becomes a screen in a group.

The result

Inspiration becomes material.

A screen you admired on Monday is a decomposed, editable, linkable stack by Tuesday — sitting beside your frame, one cut at a time, feeding real work.
