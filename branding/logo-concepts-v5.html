New concepts · v5 six more orphans

Round five: six more metaphors, untouched until now.

Signal processing, physics, occlusion, the editor's own chrome, isometric space, and layers. Same palette, same law — the focused thing is the only thing that's lit.

D1Signal

Focus as signal-to-noise ratio: a spectrum of low, flat, cool noise — and one frequency that isn't noise at all, but a component standing at full amplitude. Attention is literally a weighting over a distribution, and here the distribution is drawn with one weight near 1.0 and the rest near zero.

the peak is a component (rounded, warm, wide), not a bar. Make it a bar and it becomes every audio app ever shipped.

D2Lift

The most physical of the set: components sit on a shelf, and the selected one has been picked up — levitating, lit, with its warm light spilling back onto the shelf below. The gap between the shelf and the component is the entire message: editing means lifting something out of its resting place.

the amber pool of light on the shelf under the lifted component. Remove it and the levitation becomes a layout accident.

D3Blinds

Occlusion with rhythm: horizontal slats cover the whole surface, and they part only where the component is. Where Keyhole (v4) carved one hole in a slab, Blinds turns the cover into a repeating texture — calmer, more architectural, and instantly recognizable in silhouette.

only the three middle slats split; the outer ones run unbroken. Split them all and the structure dissolves into stripes.

D4Marquee

The editor's own chrome, promoted to logo: a selection marquee — marching-ants dashes, eight handles, rotation stem — wrapped around the component. It's the only concept where the mark is literally a screenshot of the product's core interaction. Honest to a fault.

the rotation handle floating above. It's what separates "design tool selection" from "generic crop icon".

D5Elevation

An isometric grid of flat, dim cells — and one cell extruded into a solid block, its top face the only lit surface in the scene. Focus as elevation: the component doesn't just glow, it gains a dimension the rest of the world doesn't have. It also quietly rhymes with a digital-twin, warehouse-grid way of seeing software.

the cell directly behind the block stays dimmer than its siblings — the block casts an absence, not a shadow.

D6Peel

The canvas is a cover, and its corner has been peeled back — underneath, on the layer below, the component glows. Focus as undressing the surface: you don't navigate to the component, you remove everything that isn't it. The fold is frozen mid-gesture, like the page is still moving.

the fold triangle is the mirrored corner, geometrically exact. Eyeball it and the paper physics break — and so does the mark.

Where each one shines: Signal is the cleanest one-glance read and scales beautifully. Lift has the most charm — tactile, friendly, the one users would describe with a smile. Blinds owns the strongest pure silhouette of this round. Marquee is the most product-honest and doubles as UI iconography for free. Elevation is the one with a second life: it could brand the simulation/digital-twin side of your world as easily as the editor. Peel is the most narrative and begs to be animated. Across all five docs, the serious shortlist now looks like: Iris (+trail), Extraction, Keyhole, Blinds, and Elevation — five genuinely different identities for the same idea. Round six exists if you want it, but the next interesting document is probably the one where we eliminate.
