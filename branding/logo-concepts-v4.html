New concepts · v4 six orphans, no families

Six new concepts. No variations, no states — each one stands alone.

Same palette, same rule — only the focused thing is lit — but six metaphors not touched yet: negative space, typography, recursion, the attention diagram itself, resolution, and gravity.

C1Keyhole

Pure negative space: a solid slab covers everything, and the only opening is component-shaped. The light doesn't sit on the surface — it comes from behind, through the cutout. The anti-canvas stated as architecture: there is no surface to wander, only one way in.

the registration ticks at the cutout corners — crop marks claiming "this is the artboard now".

C2Glyph ƒ

The only lettermark in the whole exploration. A lowercase ƒ stem with its crossbar replaced by components: the lit one docked on the left, a dim stub on the right. It reads as a letter at distance and as a component rail up close — and ƒ is also math's function symbol, which suits a tool built on composition.

the asymmetric crossbar — one side lit and large, the other dim and small. Balance it and the letter dies.

C3Corridor

Focus as recursion: frames nest toward an off-center vanishing point, each level closer to the thing you're editing — page, section, component, the component. It's your drill-down navigation drawn literally, and the off-center pull keeps it from collapsing into a generic target.

the vanishing point sits at the lower right, never the center. Centered, it's a bullseye; off-center, it's a journey.

C4Flow

The attention mechanism itself: three inputs — read them as query, key, value — feed one output, and the line weights are the weights. One connection dominates, the others whisper. The most direct homage to the paper in any document so far; it's basically Figure 2 reduced to a mark.

the three different stroke weights. Equalize them and it becomes any integration-platform logo.

C5Resolve

Focus as resolution: the world is an undifferentiated dot field — low-res, unloaded, ambient — and one region has resolved into a crisp, solid component. The dots immediately bordering it sharpen slightly, like an image snapping into focus from the center out.

the brightened halo ring of dots around the component — the gradient of sharpness, not a hard edge.

C6Orbit

Attention as gravity: components spiral inward, shrinking, tilting, and warming as they approach the center — the last one already glows amber, about to merge. The focused component isn't selected, it attracts. The slight rotation on each satellite is what sells the motion.

the spiral's decay — size, distance, and tilt all shrink together. A circular ring of satellites is the cliché this refuses to be.

Where each one shines: Keyhole is the boldest and most ownable silhouette — negative space ages best. Glyph ƒ is the only one that gives you a letter, useful the day the app gets its real name (any name keeping an "f" inherits it). Corridor maps one-to-one to your drill-down UX. Flow is the purest paper homage and the best conversation starter. Resolve is the most atmospheric, born for splash screens. Orbit has the most life and implies motion without moving. Against the earlier docs: Keyhole and Glyph ƒ are the two that compete seriously with Iris and Extraction — the others are strong as supporting illustrations even if they don't become the mark.
