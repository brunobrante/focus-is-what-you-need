Variations · v3 iris × extraction

Iris and Extraction, pushed in every direction they can bend.

Two families from the two strongest marks. Iris variations explore the aperture's states — open, closed, rotated, wireframed. Extraction variations explore composition and reduction — vertical, glyph-only, monoline. At the end, the two ideas merge.

Family 1 · Iris

The aperture has states, and each state is a usable asset: closed for loading, base for the icon, open for the moment a component enters edit mode. The blades never form a closed square — the pinwheel gaps are the family's protected detail.

A1Base

The reference state. Blade overshoot gives it rotation; the opening is component-shaped, not circular.

A2Open

Blades pulled back, component at full size. The "editing now" state — end frame of the launch animation.

A3Closed

Nearly shut, a sliver of warmth inside. Loading state, notification badge, or the "nothing selected" empty state.

A4Diamond

The same geometry rotated 45°. More dynamic, more "tool-like" — reads almost as a shutter mid-spin.

A5Wireframe

Blades reduced to thin off-white strokes. The light-touch version for watermarks, footers, and UI chrome.

A6Mono / paper

One ink, two opacities. Survives print, laser engraving, and a fax machine, if you ever meet one.

Family 2 · Extraction (the original "focus" mark)

The v1 story — parent, empty slot, extracted child under brackets — recomposed for different containers. The open corner and the three-dot attention ramp stay constant; everything else flexes.

B1Base

The original diagonal composition. Still the fullest telling of the story.

B2Stacked

Vertical recomposition: the child drops out of the parent's bottom edge, through the open gap, straight into focus. Centered and symmetric — calmer than the diagonal.

B3Glyph

Cells and dots removed, strokes thickened. The favicon and small-size workhorse: open frame, lit square, brackets.

B4Monoline

Single stroke weight, single color, one solid fill. Built for embroidery, stickers, and merch where gradients die.

B5Light tile

The light-mode app icon. Structure fades toward the background; the child keeps the only saturated color on the tile.

B6Mono / paper

Ink-only counterpart of B1, matched to A6 so both families share a print system.

Hybrids · Iris × Extraction

The two stories are compatible: extraction explains where the component comes from, the iris explains what happens when it arrives.

H1Iris + brackets

Extraction's viewfinder brackets live inside the aperture, hugging the component. Two layers of focus — optical and UI — in one mark. The densest option, best at large sizes.

H2Iris + trail

The three attention dots slip in through the pinwheel's corner gap — the component arriving into the aperture. Keeps Iris's clean silhouette, inherits Extraction's softmax wink, and the corner gap suddenly has a job. The strongest merge.

Finalists at size

B2 · stacked

Recommendation: run a two-mark system. H2 (Iris + trail) as the brand mark and app icon — cleanest silhouette, survives 20 px, carries both metaphors. B1/B2 (Extraction) as the expressive mark for marketing, empty states, and onboarding, where its fuller story has room to breathe. A6 and B6 cover print as one ink system. The launch animation writes itself: dots travel in, blades open, brackets snap.
