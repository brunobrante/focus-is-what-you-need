working name → focus-is-what-you-need // after "Attention Is All You Need"

No infinite canvas. One component leaves the structure, gets pulled into the viewfinder, and becomes the only thing that's lit.

The mark tells the product's whole story in one frame: an organized parent, docked siblings, an open slot, and the extracted child under focus brackets.

Anatomy — why it's hard to copy

01The structure

A parent component with its children docked in a grid — the anti-canvas. One corner of the frame is deliberately open: a slot is empty because its component is out being edited. The broken outline is the signature detail; generic "grid" logos never sacrifice the frame.

02The attention ramp

Three dots escape through the open corner, growing in size and opacity — attention weights ramping toward 1.0. It's the quiet nod to "Attention Is All You Need": softmax made visible. Decorative to most eyes, a wink to anyone who's read the paper.

03The focused component

The extracted child sits inside viewfinder brackets — the universal symbol for "locked focus" — and carries the only warm color in the system. The palette itself encodes the product rule: everything cool and dim except what you're working on.
